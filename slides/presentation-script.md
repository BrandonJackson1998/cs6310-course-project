### Slide 1 — Title (~0:45)

**Say:**

“I’m presenting work from Tipping and Bishop’s 1999 JRSS Paper B article on **Probabilistic Principal Component Analysis**. The probabilistic story is a **linear Gaussian latent factor model**: each observation is generated by a low-dimensional latent vector mapped through a loading matrix, plus Gaussian noise with **scalar** variance sigma squared on every dimension.”

**Refs:** **[TB99]** — They introduce PPCA precisely as this latent-variable formulation and derive inference/fitting procedures from it.

---

### Slide 2 — Problem & paper overview (~1:30)

**Say:**

“In classical PCA we seek directions that capture maximal variance—equivalently the **orthogonal projection** that minimizes squared reconstruction error on **centered** data. That gives a powerful geometric recipe, but PCA itself doesn’t package signal and noise into a single **likelihood**. PPCA upgrades the story: observations arise from latent factors plus **isotropic** Gaussian noise. That buys you a joint density for **x**, principled estimation of noise variance alongside subspace parameters, and EM when you treat latent codes as missing data—ideas **[TB99]** develop fully.”

**Refs:** **[TB99]** — Motivation is tying PCA-like dimension reduction to **maximum likelihood** under explicit noise.

---

### Slide 3 — Main ideas from the paper **[expand optional]** (~1:15)

**Say:**

“They show maximum likelihood connects cleanly to **principal components** of the sample covariance—the fitted signal subspace aligns with PCA’s leading eigenvectors under their model class. Joint estimation of **sigma squared** distinguishes PPCA from treating residuals informally after PCA. They also give an **EM algorithm** acting as if latent **z** were missing data; stationary points correspond to the same likelihood landscape as the spectral estimator up to rotation of factors—a standard identifiability caveat.”

**Refs:** **[TB99]** — Spectral / EM dual derivation is core contribution.

---

### Slide 4–5 — Method summary (~2:00)

**Say:**

“Generatively: sample **z** from a unit Gaussian in q dimensions, multiply by **W**, add mean **mu**, add noise **epsilon ~ N(0, sigma² I)**. Learning uses either **closed-form** spectral estimates from the covariance structure **[TB99]** or **EM**, alternating posterior inference for **z given x** with regression-style updates for **W** and sigma squared **[TB99]**. Our slides omit algebra; the paper carries it.”

**Refs:** **[TB99]** — Full likelihood and update equations.

### Slide 6 — Implementation (~1:15)

**Say:**

“We implemented three aligned estimators for comparison: **PCA** via truncated SVD on standardized data; **PPCA closed-form** matching the spectral maximum likelihood pipeline described in **[TB99]**; and **PPCA EM**, iterating until parameter updates fall below tolerance. Out-of-sample plots use PPCA’s **posterior mean** map from **x** back through **W**, consistent with treating PPCA as a generative smoother—not identical to PCA’s plain orthogonal projector.”

**Refs:** **[TB99]** EM section; **[Evidence]** — `ppca/pca.py`, `ppca/ppca_closed_form.py`, `ppca/ppca_em.py`.

### Slide 10 — Quantitative table (~1:45)

**Say:**

“At **q equals ten**, PCA reconstruction MSE on standardized Wine is about **0.0383**, while PPCA—both closed-form and EM—is about **0.0749**, a gap near **0.0366**. MNIST shows PCA **0.6092**, PPCA **0.6095**, differing only **about three ten-thousandths**. Olivetti sits essentially tied at **0.339446 versus 0.339448**. Critically, EM versus closed-form PPCA differs by at most **two point three times ten to the minus eighth** on reconstruction MSE across datasets—that’s numerical noise.”

**Refs:** **[Evidence]** — Exact rows: Wine PCA **0.0383028**, PPCA **0.0748682**, delta **0.0365653**; MNIST deltas **0.000293283**; Olivetti deltas **2.16534e-06** and EM minus CF **2.32302e-08**.

**Say (interpretation tie-in):**

“That PCA beats PPCA here on reconstruction MSE does **not** contradict **[TB99]**—PCA is tuned for orthogonal reconstruction loss on centered data, whereas PPCA here scores posterior-mean reconstruction under its likelihood **[Evidence]** narrative in `experiment_log.md`. Different objectives can disagree even when subspaces overlap.”
